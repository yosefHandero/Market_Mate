# Execution Roadmap

## Phase 1: Prove The Core Loop

### Goal

Freeze one primary strategy loop so evidence becomes interpretable.

### Deliverables

- product thesis locked to decision support and validation first
- one primary strategy contract
- one primary horizon
- one benchmark ladder
- one evidence review cadence

### Exit Criteria

- strategy changes stop moving the goalposts mid-window
- evidence packs become the default review artifact
- supportive providers are treated as optional until proven

## Phase 2: Harden Paper Trading

### Goal

Make the paper loop trustworthy enough to judge both system quality and operator quality.

### Deliverables

- repeatable paper-trading operating checklist
- consistent use of previews and dry-run audits
- explicit journal action discipline
- better visibility into readiness, stale scans, and trust immaturity

### Exit Criteria

- operator can reconstruct signal to action to outcome consistently
- paper sessions run without silent freshness or audit failures
- weekly reviews can distinguish system issues from operator issues

## Phase 3: Validate Edge With Evidence

### Goal

Prove whether the current loop has durable, friction-adjusted edge.

### Deliverables

- walk-forward validation windows
- benchmark reporting
- provider ablation comparisons
- strategy-version scorecards

### Exit Criteria

- holdout expectancy is positive after friction
- passed `BUY` and `SELL` cohorts are sufficiently sampled
- results survive regime and asset-type segmentation

## Phase 4: Prepare Monetization

### Goal

Package the strongest proven workflow into something a buyer can understand and trust.

### Deliverables

- one primary buyer persona
- one narrow promise
- trust and limitation language
- buyer-facing proof pack

### Exit Criteria

- the product can be pitched without implying live-trading certainty
- a buyer can understand why the system is worth paying for now

## Phase 5: Controlled Automation

### Goal

Add tiny, reversible automation only after paper evidence and operations are credible.

### Deliverables

- broker-native reconciliation
- live-vs-paper drift analysis
- explicit promotion rules for capital size
- incident runbooks and kill switches

### Exit Criteria

- live automation remains tightly capped
- failures are detectable and reversible
- the operator can explain both the edge case and the safety case
